Privacy Policy

Last updated: December 23, 2025

HLIN (“we”, “us”, or “our”) respects your privacy and is committed to protecting your personal data. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, and protect information when you use the HLIN mobile application and website (together, the “Service”).

By accessing or using HLIN, you agree to the practices described in this Privacy Policy. If you do not agree, please do not use the Service.

1. Information We Collect

We design HLIN to collect only what is necessary to operate, secure, and improve the Service. We do not build personal profiles for advertising or sell your data.

1.1 Information You Provide

  • Account information (if/when accounts are supported), such as email address, password (stored in encrypted form), and any optional profile details you choose to provide.
  • Subscription and billing information, which is processed by third‑party payment providers (for example, Apple or other processors). We do not store full payment card numbers on our own servers.

1.2 Information Collected Automatically

  • Device and app data, such as device type, operating system version, app version, and basic technical identifiers used for app performance and crash diagnostics.
  • Crash and performance analytics, including crash logs, error messages, and approximate session details, so we can understand app stability and performance.
  • Audio interaction data, such as which audio sessions are started, paused, and completed; timestamps and playback duration; and aggregated engagement metrics used to understand which content is most used.

These analytics are processed in a way that is not intended to identify you personally and are used in aggregate or pseudonymous form.

2. How We Use Your Information

We use the information we collect to:

  • Provide and operate the Service, including audio streaming and subscription management.
  • Monitor performance, reliability, and stability of the app.
  • Diagnose and fix crashes, errors, and bugs.
  • Understand which features and audio content are being used so we can improve the listening experience.
  • Protect against abuse, fraud, and security threats.
  • Comply with legal obligations and App Store requirements.

We do not use your listening data to personally profile you or for targeted advertising.

3. Analytics and Crash Reporting

We use third‑party analytics and crash reporting providers, including Google services, to understand how HLIN performs and where it may fail.

These services may receive:

  • Device information (for example, model and operating system version).
  • App version and technical identifiers.
  • Anonymous usage and crash data related to performance and stability.

We configure these tools so that data is used in aggregate and is not targeted at specific individuals.

4. Cookies and Similar Technologies

If you use the HLIN website, we may use cookies or similar technologies to remember basic settings and to measure aggregate site traffic and performance.

You can control cookies through your browser settings. Disabling cookies may affect some website functionality.

5. Data Sharing and Disclosure

We do not sell your personal data. We may share limited information with:

  • Service providers (such as hosting providers, analytics providers, crash reporting tools, and payment processors) only to the extent necessary to provide and maintain the Service.
  • Authorities or other parties when required by law, regulation, legal process, or governmental request, or to enforce our terms, detect or prevent fraud, or address security issues.

6. Data Retention

We retain information only for as long as necessary to provide and maintain the Service, comply with legal, accounting, or reporting obligations, and resolve disputes or enforce our agreements.

Crash and analytics data are typically stored in aggregated or pseudonymous form and retained according to the policies of the providers we use.

7. Your Rights and Choices

Depending on your jurisdiction, you may have rights regarding your personal data, including the right to request access, correction, deletion, or restriction of certain processing.

If you wish to exercise any of these rights, please contact us using the details in the “Contact Us” section below. We may need to verify your identity before responding.

On your device, you may also limit analytics or crash reporting using operating system settings where available, and you can always uninstall the app to stop any further data collection from that device.

8. Children’s Privacy

HLIN is not directed to children under the age required by local law (for example, 13 in the United States or 16 in parts of the European Union), and we do not knowingly collect personal data from children in that age group.

If you believe a child has provided us with personal data, please contact us so we can take appropriate steps to delete such information.

9. International Data Transfers

Our service providers and servers may be located in different countries. This means your data may be processed outside your country of residence, where data protection laws may be different.

Where required, we take steps to ensure that appropriate safeguards are in place for such transfers.

10. Security

We use reasonable technical and organizational measures to help protect your information from unauthorized access, loss, misuse, or alteration, including encryption in transit where appropriate and restricting access to authorized personnel.

No system is completely secure, but protecting your privacy and data is a core priority for HLIN.

11. Third‑Party Links

HLIN may contain links to third‑party websites or services (for example, social channels or payment providers). Their own privacy policies govern how they handle your data, and we are not responsible for their practices.

12. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time to reflect changes in our practices, technologies, or legal requirements. When we make material changes, we will update the “Last updated” date at the top of this page and may provide additional notice within the app or on the website.
